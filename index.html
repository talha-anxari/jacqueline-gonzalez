About the Author

Jacqueline Gonzalez

Ridge & Coast Writer

Jacqueline Gonzalez grew up at the edge of the orchard, where the mountains held their own weather and the ridge seemed to go on forever.

Her work explores the quiet distances between home and horizon — the landscapes we leave behind and the ones that never leave us. She lives near the water now, but she still writes from the ridge.

"I write to find my way back to the ridge, even when I'm standing by the sea."

About the Book

Some distances aren't measured in miles.

Eli grew up on the ridge, where the orchard trees were older than anyone's memory and the mountains kept their own weather. The water was always somewhere else — a place people left for, not a place anyone came back from.

From the Ridge to the Water follows one slow season of leaving: a daughter, a falling-down farmhouse, and the long, unhurried walk toward the coast her mother never finished.

— Jacqueline Gonzalez
